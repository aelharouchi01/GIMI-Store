Our Organization

About GIMI

The Global Innovation Management Institute (GIM Institute) is a global, nonprofit organization with a mission to make innovation a management discipline.

1.1

Mission & History

Founded in 2009 by a worldwide group of chief innovation officers, academics, and consultants, GIMI became the first global standard certification board for innovation management incorporating ISO standards. Our mission is to democratize innovation worldwide by certifying one million individuals and companies.

Our work spans 60+ countries, 140+ universities, and 1,200+ corporations. In 2023, GIMI merged with the International Association of Innovation Professionals (IAOIP), establishing the largest professional organization on innovation worldwide.

The Certification

Everything you need to know about the Certified Innovation Professional - Level 1 certification: eligibility, examination, and the application journey.

2.1

Scope & Prerequisites

Scope. This page covers the Certified Innovation Professional - Level 1 scheme only. This certification validates foundational knowledge of innovation fundamentals, tools, techniques, and processes for breakthrough innovations. Other GIMI certifications are administered separately and fall outside the scope of this accredited program.

Eligibility requirements:

Template – awaiting confirmation

  • No formal educational prerequisite is required for the Certified Innovation Professional - Level 1 examination
  • Candidates must be 18 years of age or older
  • Professional experience in a business, academic, or organizational role is recommended but not mandatory
  • All candidates must agree to the GIMI Code of Conduct before sitting the examination

There is no mandatory training requirement. Candidates may self-study using the published examination blueprint and competency framework.

Note: Completing a training program is not required to sit for this examination and confers no advantage in the certification process.

Certification Cycle
3 years from the effective date of certification
CPE Requirement
60 hours of Continuing Professional Education (CPE) must be completed and documented over the 3-year certification cycle
What Counts as CPE

Template – awaiting confirmation

  • Attending recognized innovation conferences, seminars, or workshops
  • Completing online courses in innovation management or related fields
  • Delivering presentations or lectures on innovation topics at recognized events
  • Publishing articles or research in innovation management
  • Participating in GIMI community events or webinars

Impartiality

GIMI's certification body operates independently from training. The information below explains how impartiality is structured and protected.

Our Impartiality Commitment

The GIMI certification body is responsible for the impartiality of its certification activities. We do not allow commercial, financial, or other pressures to compromise that impartiality. Training is not required to sit for our certification examinations.

4.1

Impartiality Policy

Top management commitment:

Financial independence:

All Certified Innovation Professional - Level 1 certification fees are paid directly to GIMI. Fees are not collected by or channelled through training partners. The public fee schedule is available in the Apply & Fees section above.

Independence of certification decisions:

Personnel who make certification decisions for the Certified Innovation Professional - Level 1 scheme have not participated in training or examining that specific candidate.

4.2

Separation from Training

Preparation training is available through GIMI Certified Training Partners (CTPs) as an optional pathway. Completing a training program is not a prerequisite for the Certified Innovation Professional - Level 1 examination and confers no advantage in the certification process.

Structural controls separating training from certification for this scheme:

  • The certification body operates under governance that is independent of the training function
  • All Level 1 certification fees are paid directly to GIMI, not through training partners
  • This certification page contains no links to training partner offers or course sales
  • Certified Training Partner agreements include impartiality obligations and are subject to ongoing monitoring
  • Certification decisions are made by personnel who have not trained or examined the candidate in question
