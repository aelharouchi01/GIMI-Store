Overview

Measure your readiness to innovate.

A 45-minute self-assessment that looks beyond job title to answer one useful question: how ready are you, right now, to turn ideas into outcomes?

The Innovation Potential Assessment (IPA) gauges the readiness of an individual to initiate and implement innovation projects. It measures two dimensions, your innovation mindset and your knowledge and tools, then maps you to one of seven innovation profiles.

The result is an automated report that benchmarks your strengths, surfaces what is holding you back, and recommends the training that will move you forward fastest. For teams, the same assessment rolls up into an organization-wide view of collective innovation potential.

At a glance

  • ~45-minute online self-assessment
  • Two dimensions: Mindset + Knowledge & Tools
  • Maps you to one of 7 innovation profiles
  • Automated report with a personalized growth path

What It Measures

Two dimensions of innovation potential.

Innovation takes more than good ideas. It takes the right mindset to pursue them and the right tools to execute. The IPA scores both, and each is broken down into four components.

Dimension 1

Innovation Mindset

The attitudes and behaviors that determine whether you pursue and persist with innovation.

What it covers
  • Growth Mindset · the belief that ability can be developed
  • Grit · persistence through setbacks and ambiguity
  • Creative Collaboration · building on others to create value
  • Flexible Execution · adapting as conditions change
Dimension 2

Knowledge & Tools

Your command of the methods that carry an idea from intent to a real, implemented outcome.

What it covers
  • Intent · setting a clear innovation direction
  • Concepts · shaping opportunities into strong concepts
  • Resources · mobilizing what you need to deliver
  • Make it Real · executing and commercializing

Innovation Profiles

Seven profiles, one personalized journey.

Your scores place you in one of seven innovation profiles. Each profile comes with a customized journey map showing where to focus to become a stronger innovator, so the assessment ends with a direction, not just a number.

Rule Challenger

Creative, forward-thinking A-player with the attitude to learn by doing and an eagerness to build the new.

Rule Breaker

Creative, forward-thinking learner and experimenter who continuously pivots to build the new.

Rule Maker

Experienced thought leader who courageously challenges the status quo and executes powerful value propositions.

Diamond in the Rough

Sharp thinker who is open to learn and has strong foundations to foster innovation potential.

Optimistic Executor

Result-driven executor who brings ideas together in a fast, flexible, and disciplined approach.

Rule Executor

Brings specific know-how to innovation teams, and needs short-term results to gain confidence to build the new.

Rule Follower

Accepts established practices and prefers to follow rules and guidelines to execute activities.
