Overview

Strengthen the system that scales startups.

The world's first ISO-aligned assessment and certification built for the organizations that help founders scale.

The Venture Ecosystem Assessment (VEA) is the world's first ISO-aligned innovation assessment and certification framework, built specifically for incubators, accelerators, innovation hubs, and startup ecosystem organizations. It evaluates the institutional systems that enable founders and startups to scale successfully.

The framework helps these organizations support more scalable startups, demonstrate measurable impact, benchmark performance globally, and build sustainable innovation ecosystems.

At a glance

  • First ISO-aligned ecosystem framework
  • Four evaluation dimensions
  • Certified across four maturity levels
  • Global benchmarking and a growth roadmap
  • Leadership interviews and a systems review
For incubators, accelerators, innovation hubs, and startup ecosystems.

What We Evaluate

Four dimensions, thirteen subcomponents.

Built on GIMI's updated High-Performance Innovation (HPI) Framework, endorsed as ISO 56002+ aligned, the VEA scores your ecosystem across four dimensions and thirteen subcomponents.

Innovation Strategy

A clear innovation vision aligned with founder needs, ecosystem priorities, and market opportunities.

  • Purpose & Case for Change
  • Focus Areas
  • Portfolio & Pipeline Management

Innovation Capacity

The operational capability to consistently support startup growth and venture development.

  • Programme Process & Curriculum
  • Team, Mentors & Partner Network
  • Resources & Funding
  • Community & Alumni Engagement

Innovation Discipline

The governance, leadership, and cultural systems to operate effectively at scale.

  • Governance & Leadership
  • Entrepreneurial Culture & Learning
  • Metrics & Recognition

Innovation Performance

How effectively the hub translates activity into measurable startup and ecosystem outcomes.

  • Meaningful Change & Impact
  • Value Capture & Sustainability
  • AI Capability

Maturity Levels

From Emerging Incubator to Ecosystem Leader.

Certification charts how far an ecosystem's systems have matured, and where to grow next.

1
Level 1
Emerging Incubator
The foundations are set: a defined curriculum, dedicated leadership, and formal partnerships give ventures something solid to build on.
2
Level 2
Structured Builder
Repeatable processes and structures support startups consistently, cohort after cohort and partner by partner.
3
Level 3
Advanced Scaler
A programme that delivers: diversified portfolio, evidenced exits, an active alumni network, and governance that holds up to scrutiny.
4
Level 4
Ecosystem Leader
The benchmark others measure against: proprietary deal flow, AI-enabled operations, self-sustaining finances, and ventures reshaping markets.

Each level corresponds to a formal GIMI certificate, demonstrating that your incubator or accelerator operates with globally benchmarked innovation systems and ecosystem capabilities.

How It Works

What the assessment includes.

1
Organizational Assessment
Evaluation across strategy, founder support systems, governance, culture, operations, and innovation performance.
2
Leadership & Ecosystem Interviews
Strategic discussions with leadership, mentors, partners, founders, and ecosystem stakeholders.
3
Systems & Evidence Review
Analysis of incubation methodologies, acceleration frameworks, governance, operations, partnerships, and impact models.
4
Strategic Outcomes
Maturity benchmarking, gap analysis, strategic recommendations, certification guidance, and an institutional growth roadmap.
