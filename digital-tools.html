Overview

Run innovation with structure, not spreadsheets.

Good ideas get lost without a system. GIMI's tools capture, refine, and prioritize them, and give leaders a live view of the whole pipeline.

Used on their own or alongside GIMI programs, these platforms bring a standardized, diverge-and-converge process to innovation: explore the future, gather ideas from across the organization and beyond, and turn the best of them into a managed portfolio of concepts, with the dashboards leaders need to steer.

In Detail

Crowdsourcing & Service Mapping, in practice.

Two of GIMI's platforms have been deployed at scale, with a structured process, clear roles, and live dashboards behind each.

Crowdsourcing Platform

Deployed with a leading bank to crowdsource concepts across the whole organization and feed them into its innovation pursuit program.

The platform runs a structured diverge-and-converge process: participants work first individually, then in pairs, then as teams, moving from idea fragments to refined business concepts. An innovation committee makes the final selection, and gamified dashboards monitor performance, nudge participants, and summarize outcomes, all backed by trained coaches and mentors.

How it works

  • Leadership sets the innovation intent and focus areas
  • Diverge: crowdsource idea fragments at scale
  • Converge: refine and prioritize into concepts
  • Individual → pairs → team progression
  • Gamified dashboards and trained coaches
Goes beyond individuals to teams, fragments to concepts, and one-time events to systems thinking.

Service Mapping

Deployed with the Qatar Government to identify and prioritize the public services most worth improving for citizens.

Each governmental entity uses the software to complete a structured, three-step process: build an entity profile, run a service audit using journey maps from both the entity and service-user perspectives, and prioritize the challenges worth solving. Entity heads validate the final report, and dashboards roll the results up into a government-wide view of service performance.

Three-step process

  • Entity Profile · scope and leadership commitment
  • Service Audit · journey maps, entity and user views
  • Prioritization · rank the challenges worth solving
  • Entity head validates the final report
  • Dashboards aggregate government-wide performance
Turns service improvement into a structured, data-driven discipline.
