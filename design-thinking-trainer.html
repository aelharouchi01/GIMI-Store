The Program

Solve complex problems, then teach your teams to do the same.

Design Thinking is a repeatable, human-centered process for turning real user needs into tested, implementable solutions. This program certifies you to both apply it and lead it, so the capability compounds across everyone you train.

Across three stages, Immerse, Invent, and Implement, you learn to move a vague challenge to a validated solution: understanding users in depth, framing the right problem, generating and prototyping ideas, and testing your way to something that works. GIMI is the global nonprofit that sets the standards for innovation management, with 18,000+ professionals certified across the Fortune 500.

The Curriculum

Three stages, six disciplines, one iterative loop.

Every idea is pressure-tested against three questions before it moves forward.

Desirable

Do users actually want it?

Feasible

Can it be built?

Viable

Does it work for the business?

Stage One · Immerse

Understand the problem

Get close to users before deciding what to build.

1 · Understand

Engage directly with users to build empathy and surface the tacit needs people cannot put into words.

2 · Map

Synthesize observations into clear patterns, pain points, and a sharp view of where the opportunities lie.

Stage Two · Invent

Define and create

Frame the right problem, then generate solutions worth building.

1 · Define

Reframe the challenge into a precise, evidence-based problem statement, so the team solves the right problem.

2 · Ideate

Generate a wide range of solutions through divergent thinking, then converge on the ideas with the strongest potential.

Stage Three · Implement

Bring ideas to life

Make ideas tangible and let evidence guide every iteration.

1 · Prototype

Build quick, low-cost mock-ups of your best ideas so they can reach real users fast.

2 · User Test

Gather honest feedback from users and stakeholders, and let evidence guide the next iteration.

3 · Iterate

Cycle back through Immerse and Invent as testing reveals what the work still needs.

↻ Design Thinking is a loop, not a line, cycle back through the stages as often as the problem demands.

Two levels to certification

Level 1 · Foundation

Build a strong command of Design Thinking across all three stages, plus the facilitation skills to guide a team through them.

Level 2 · Application

Apply the method to a real organizational challenge and document your findings and outcomes in a white paper.
